About this Seva

Bhandara means "storehouse" in Sanskrit — a generous community feast where food is offered freely to all who arrive. In the spiritual tradition, organizing a Bhandara in a holy dham like Vrindavan or Mathura carries extraordinary merit. Our team prepares sattvic (pure vegetarian) meals under hygienic conditions and serves them to devotees at sacred locations.

Every Bhandara begins with a sankalp — a sacred intention — recited by our Brahmin pandits, naming you and your family as the sponsor. Your intention, whether for the welfare of a departed soul, to celebrate a birth or wedding, or simply as an offering of gratitude, is formally acknowledged in the divine presence.

Benefits

Meritorious karma (Punya)
Feeding the hungry in a holy dham is one of the highest meritorious acts in Sanatan Dharma, creating powerful spiritual merit for your family.
Pitru Tarpan (Ancestral Blessings)
A Bhandara performed in the name of departed souls is believed to grant them peace and liberation. Particularly auspicious on shraddha tithi.
Shared divine joy
The smiles of hundreds of devotees receiving food in Krishna's dham carry their own blessings back to you as the donor.
Complete transparency
You receive photo and video proof of your Seva within 24 hours — the only platform that offers this level of documented accountability.
